rPanglaoDB Application Case: Fibrocytes

To show the potential of rPanglaoDB for collecting rare cell types by integrating multiple public datasets, we present a biological application collecting and characterizing a set of fibrocytes.

Fibrocytes are marrow-derived cells that can differentiate into fibroblasts from monocyte precursors. These cells exhibit both the inflammatory features of macrophages and the tissue remodeling properties of fibroblasts. They are present at wound sites where they participate in tissue injury repair and remodeling [21597472]. Due to the lack of an unbiased characterization of their transcriptome, their marker genes are missed in all the databases used by the tools designed to assign cell-types automatically from single-cell RNA-seq data such as MSigDB [21546393], ClusterMole, and PanglaoDB [30951143]. Fibrocytes differentiate from fibroblasts and macrophages by expressing CD34 [8790603, 19834619]. They differentiate also from macrophages by expressing ACTA2, FAP, FN1, COL5A1, COL5A2, COL5A3, CD90, and the lack of CSF1R, and also differentiate from fibroblasts by expressing CHIT1, MME, SIRPA, PTPRC, and ICAM1 among others [21597472].

Using the getMarkers function, we identified a cluster of cells in the SRS3121028 sample derived from skin wound tissues (3 days after scab detachment) expressing CD34, ACTA2, FN1, Collagen V, FAP, SIRPA and the lack of expression of CSF1R. Genes that differentiate fibrocytes from macrophages and fibroblasts [30464171](Lim, et al., 2018; Pilling, et al., 2009; Reilkoff, et al., 2011).

Since, fibrocytes are marrow-derived cells that differentiate into fibroblasts-like phenotypes, they are usually wrong labeled as fibroblasts. Thus, we downloaded using the getSamples function all the fibroblasts available from dermis samples in the database (SRA accessions: SRS3121028 and SRS3121030) [30464171].
